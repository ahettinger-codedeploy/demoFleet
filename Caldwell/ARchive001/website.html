Principals of the Gramercy Brass
Friday, October 17, 2008 8:00 p.m. Alumni Theatre

Led by founder and music director, John Henry Lambert, the Principals of the award winning Gramercy Brass Orchestra of New York will perform a concert of brass chamber works including selections from their new CD, “Brubeck in Brass” to be released in 2009. As ensemble in residence, the members of The Gramercy Brass Orchestra frequently perform at Caldwell and offer annual workshops and a week long summer camp for young brass players. For more information about Gramercy Brass at Caldwell, go to http://www.gramercybrass.com/.

Garden State Opera*
Sunday, November 16, 2008 – 4:00 p.m. Student Center
Free performance of excerpts for schools: Friday, November 14, 2008 –1:00 p.m. Student Center

Don Pasquale by Gaetano Donizetti (Sung in Italian with projected titles in English)

In Donizetti’s sunny opera buffa masterpiece, true love wins out when the old skinflint Pasquale tries to force his nephew to marry for money — and gets a lesson he’ll never forget. The production will be set in contemporary Los Angeles and performed with orchestra. The Garden State Opera performs in northern New Jersey with singers and musicians from the NYC metro area. Founder and artistic director, Francesco Santelli, has conducted many opera and orchestral performances in the U.S. and Italy. For more information go to http://www.gardenstateopera.org/.

*This program is made possible in part by funds from the New Jersey State Council on the Arts/Department of State, a Partner Agency of the National Endowment for the Arts and administered by the Essex County Division of Cultural and Historic Affairs.

Christmas Spectacular
Friday, December 5, 2008 – 8:00 p.m. Student Center Auditorium

Join the Caldwell College Choir and Wind Ensemble to celebrate the holiday season! The Caldwell College Choir will perform holiday selections under the direction of Laura Greenwald. The Caldwell College Wind Ensemble, under the direction of Rebecca Vega will perform seasonal music concluding with our annual carol sing along!

Chris Washburne and SYOTOS Latin Jazz Band in Concert
Thursday, February 12, 2009 – 8:00 p.m. Alumni Theatre

Pre-concert discussion: 7:00 p.m. Chris Washburne and the SYOTOS Band push the genre of Latin Jazz into uncharted waters weaving contemporary uptempo beats with a driving sound, or as the New York Times states “SYOTOS plays with fire with erudition!” Syotos has become what TimeOUT New York calls a “Latin Jazz institution.” They have the longest running Latin jazz gig in the City’s history, playing every week for 18 years. For more information go to: www.chriswashburne.com/syotos2.html.

Paul Meyers, Solo Guitar, Brazilian Music and Jazz
Wednesday, March 11, 2009 – 8:00 p.m. Alumni Theatre

Guitarist/composer Paul Meyers has performed and/or recorded with many artists including Wynton Marsalis, Rufus Reid, and the Woody Herman Orchestra in appearances at the Newport Jazz Festival, Carnegie Hall and Lincoln Center. Paul’s solo work encompasses a mixture of musical styles including Be-Bop, Brazilian, Bartok, and the Blues. For more information go to http://www.paulmeyers.info/.

“One of the most eloquent jazz guitarists since Kenny Burrell.” – James Gavin, NY Times

Roseanna Vitro Sings Jazz
Master Class: Thursday, April 2, 2009, 3:00 p.m. Alumni Theatre
Concert: Friday, April 3, 2009, 8:00 p.m. Alumni Theatre

Roseanna Vitro is a storyteller with a penchant for touching audiences. A soulful communicator – expressive, melodically inventive, and hard-swinging, her collaborations cover a wide range of music and styles. Recent performances include highly acclaimed concerts at the Kennedy Center in Washington and the BlueNote jazz club in New York. Her trio includes Allen Farnham piano, Dean Johnson bass, and Tim Horner drums. For more information go to http://www.roseannavitro.com/.

NewYorkestra — Contemporary Jazz Big Band
Sunday, April 26, 2009, 3:00 p.m. Student Center Upper Lounge

NewYorkestra’s repertoire features the compositions and arrangements of bandleaders Rob Middleton and Steve Kenyon, both award winning jazz composers. Many members of NewYorkestra have played in internationally renowned bands, such as those of Maria Schneider, Toshiko Akiyoshi, Lionel Hampton, and the Vanguard Jazz Orchestra. NewYorkestra has impressed audiences and critics at performances in such New York venues the BlueNote jazz club and the 2007 Conference of the International Association for Jazz Education. For more information go to: http://www.newyorkestra.com/.

“This is a great band, supported by some great writing. Highly recommended!” – Jim McNeely
