Club Zamana is the largest undergraduate South Asian organization at Columbia University. We cater to over 500 general body members and strive to foster harmony, cultural awareness and highlight the South Asian presence at Columbia. We are an integral part of the campus and work towards bringing value not only to ourselves but to the entire community.

Zamana was founded in the 1970's as the Indian Students Association, the present name was adopted in 1987 in order to better represent the growing diversity of the South Asian population. Within the university, Zamana is recognized by both Columbia and Barnard student activity organizations. We are also members of the United Students of Color Council (USCC), the Asian American Alliance (AAA) and have ties with several academic and community leaders in the area.

Throughout the year, we hold a wide range of events for our members and the community. These events range from political forms, movie nights, community service days, semi formals and educational activities. Our latest initiative is a cultural bazaar called Rangeela that features mehndi, dance workshops, hindi writing and other South Asian themed activities. This fall we welcomed over 100 sixth graders from a local middle school to Rangeela, making the event a huge success. For the spring semester Zamana puts on a huge cultural show called Tamasha. Tamasha features performances from all three South Asian dance troupes at Columbia, other student performances and outside acts.

If you would like to learn more about these events or Zamana in general, feel free to contact us at zamana@columbia.edu. Thanks!
