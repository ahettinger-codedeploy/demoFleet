  • “I am Content to Take Thee”: Wedding preparations at Agecroft Hall: Living History Encounter Tour

    • 14 May 2016
    • 1:00 PM
    • Agecroft Hall
    • 20
    Register

    Nicholas Green is getting married! That is, if the negotiations go as planned. Join us at Agecroft Hall as we eavesdrop on the Dauntesey family and their servants as they gossip about the bride and groom, prepare food for the feast and haggle over the arrangements of the match. This is a first person living history tour of the downstairs rooms designed to entertain and educate visitors about the lives of the family who once lived at Agecroft Hall.

  • “What, Have We a Marriage Here?”: A Wedding at Agecroft Hall: Living History program

    • 14 May 2016
    • 2:00 PM
    • Agecroft Hall
    Register

    After coming to an agreement, Nicholas Green’s marriage is proceeding. Join us to see a recreation of a 17th century English wedding, and learn how important marriages were to the community.  After the “ceremony”, visitors will be invited to join the guests as they celebrate with music and dance. Weddings were a community affair, so visitors should expect to participate if they are needed!

    Program is included with Garden admission.

  • The Universe & Your (In)significant Place In It: Special Focus Tour

    • 17 May 2016
    • 6:00 PM - 7:30 PM
    • Agecroft Hall
    • 15
    Register

    Join us for Special Focus Tour:  The Universe & Your (In)significant Place In It. This tour is an overview of the personal and religious beliefs concerning the universe and the world view of people living in Tudor and Stuart England. Where do you fit in your world? How important are you in the great scheme of things? The 17th century English could easily answer these questions. The universe and your place in it were pre-arranged whether you liked it or not!

    This tour is led by Tour Guide Susan Robinson. Light refreshments follow.

    Reservations:   Reservations and advanced payment are required by Monday, May 16th.

    Fee: $8.00 per person; Free for Agecroft Hall Annual Pass holders.

  • Marye Bucke: The True Story Behind The Tempest

    • 13 Oct 2016
    • 7:00 PM
    • Agecroft Hall
    • 75
    Register

    Travel back in time to 1623 and meet with Marye Bucke, the Minister’s Wife, brought to life by historical interpreter Rebecca Suerdieck. Visit with one of the first English women in Virginia! Hear the story of her amazing journey to the New World, a journey that included a shipwreck that inspired Shakespeare's The Tempest.  Listen to true tales of life at Jamestown and of Pocahontas. Enjoy this interactive storytelling program and ask any questions you may have to someone who was actually there!

    Fee and registration: $10 per person; $7 per student. Advanced registration is appreciated but not required.
