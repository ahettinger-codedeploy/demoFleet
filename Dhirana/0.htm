About About Dhirana Competition

Dhirana is the University of Pittsburgh's very own Indian classical dance competition. Pitt first had the opportunity to host Laasya 2012 on its campus after Pitt Nrityamala won the honor by placing first at Laasya 2011. With strong support and attendance from the Pittsburgh community and enthusiastic involvement from Pitt students, Laasya 2012 was a resounding success. Pitt Nrityamala, in conjunction with the University of Pittsburgh, created Dhirana to foster community interest in Indian classical dance. This competition will provide collegiate classical dance teams with a platform to showcase their talents during a weekend experience that they will never forget.

CharityProceeds Go To Birmingham Free Clinic

“I love all of you here at the clinic. You treat me with respect, dignity and a true sense of caring and concern. Never at any medical facility have I been anything except for a number. Here, I am a name and am treated like I’m part of your family. Thank you all,” tells a patient. The Birmingham Free Clinic is a community based collaboration that provides care to the uninsured and vulnerable groups. Their goal is to provide unbiased care with dignity, compassion and understanding to all who may be in need. They provide a range of clinical services including Cardiology, Guerrilla Eye Service, Ear Nose and Throat consult services, Dermatology, pediatrics for uninsured Latino children, Smoking Cessation, Comprehensive Diabetes Clinic, and Gynecology. Each one of these services is provided by an exclusively volunteer team of physicians, pharmacists, and nurses. In addition to these services, the AmeriCorps National Service members facilitate nearly $400,000 in free medications for the uninsured patients through Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs. All of the services above continue to operate at no cost to all patients. The Birmingham Free Clinic encounters more than 3,000 patients in a year, and the number is increasing.
