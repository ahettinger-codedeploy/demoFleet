The Miracle Worker
By: William Gibson
October 8, 9 and 15-17, 2010

The Miracle Worker is the real-life story of Helen Keller, the young Alabama girl who lost her sight and hearing at the age of 19 months and whose angry and chaotic temperament is too much for her Victorian parents to handle. Luckily for them, Anne Sullivan, the 20-year-old Boston teacher is up for the challenge.

  
     

101 Dalmatians/Christmas Musical Review
Music, Lyrics by: Mel Leven, Randy Rogel, Richard Gibbs, Brian Smith, Martin Lee Fuller, Dan Root
Book Adapted & Add'l Lyrics by Marcy Heisler
Music Adapted & Arranged by Bryan Louiselle, Based on Screenplay by Bill Peet.
Based on the novel: The Hundred and One Dalmatians by Dodie Smith
December 3-5 and 10-12, 2010

Pongo and Perdita happily raise their Dalmatian puppies, until the monstrous Cruella De Vil plots to steal them for her new fur coat! Join all the dogs of London, as they daringly rescue the puppies from Cruella and her bumbling henchmen.
Following 101 Dalmatians, our CHRISTMAS MUSICAL REVIEW will showcase the talent of our youth and adult performers and fill you with Christmas spirit.

 
     

Curtains
Book by Rupert Holmes
Lyrics by Fred Ebb
Music by John Kander
Add'l Lyrics by Kander and Holmes
February 18, 19, 25-27 and March 4-6, 2011

 Set in 1946 Boston, the Tony-nominated musical comedy mystery, Curtains, features Detective Frank Cioffi. When the cast and crew of a musical become suspects after the leading lady mysteriously drops dead he is called in to solve the case.

 
     

Nobody's Perfect
By Simon Williams
April 8-9 and 15-17, 2011

Leonard, an unsuccessful novelist, knows he has a gift for writing romantic fiction and decides to enter a competition. The catch– an all female publishing house is holding it. So, under the pseudonym, Myrtle Banbury, Leonard wins the competition, but his plans seem to unravel when he must take on the identity of his creation in this fast paced comedy, Nobody’s Perfect.

 
     

The Sound of Music
Music by Richard Rodgers
Lyrics by Oscar Hammerstein II
Book by: Howard Lindsay, Russel Crouse
June 2-5 and 9-12, 2011

A spirited young woman named Maria is sent to serve as a governess for Captain Von Trapp, a widower with seven children. Her unorthodox methods quickly win over the children but clash with the regimental conduct the Captain insists upon, in the classic family musical, The Sound of Music.
